Fighter Card Experience Prototype

A structured plan and interactive preview for refactoring the fighter profile card. The goal is to surface key information at a glance while reinforcing UFC visual language through layered surfaces, typographic hierarchy, and purposeful interaction states.

Refactor Roadmap
Discovery Experience Mapping Visual Exploration Prototype & Validation Design Handoff

01 Research Foundations

  • Audit current fighter card layout, cataloging redundant or low-signal elements.
  • Pair with analytics to understand the most referenced data points pre-fight vs. post-fight.
  • Create experience goals that anchor the redesign: clarity, identity, comparability.

02 Information Architecture

  • Map an updated hierarchy balancing fighter identity, record snapshot, and matchup context.
  • Define responsive breakpoints and content rules for roster grid vs. detail contexts.
  • Document component states (default, weigh-in, fight night, post-fight recap).

03 Interaction Model

  • Prototype progressive disclosure: quick stats, expanded analytics, and narrative tags.
  • Introduce micro-interactions to reinforce state transitions and call-to-action urgency.
  • Validate accessibility targets (WCAG AA color contrast, focus order, keyboard support).

04 Visual Systems

  • Modernize typographic scale using condensed headlines paired with technical body copy.
  • Leverage glassmorphism surfaces with octagon-inspired angles for brand continuity.
  • Build tokenized color ramps for weight-class differentiation and outcome states.

05 Prototype Validation

  • Run quick usability crits with matchmakers and content editors using Figma or coded prototype.
  • Measure comprehension speed and emotional resonance through moderated sessions.
  • Finalize engineering specs: spacing tokens, component anatomy, state documentation.
Layout Anatomy
A

Hero Identity Bar: anchors the card with name, division, and record. Sticky placement enables quick scanning in multi-card grids.

B

Performance Snapshot: modular stat chips highlight the KPIs needed for matchup comparisons (reach, striking differential, finishing rate).

C

Narrative Tags: small but high-signal storytelling moments (win streak, fighting out of) that humanize the data.

D

Action Rail: houses the primary CTA and lightweight metadata (bout position, odds). Designed for high contrast and urgency.

E

State Indicator: dynamic label updates with weigh-in or fight-night context; color-coded to match broadcast graphics.

Visual Design System
Palette

Uses a cool aqua accent atop midnight neutrals. Accent ramps pair with class-specific color chips to retain UFC personality while feeling premium.

Typography

Condensed uppercase display (Inter Tight) for fighter names; geometric sans for body copy ensures readability in dense stats.

Texture

Layered glassmorphism surfaces nod to octagon panels; subtle noise gradients keep focus on content while adding depth.

Motion

Micro-interactions (hover glow, state transitions) provide immediate feedback, guiding attention without overwhelming the user.
